The real starting point

Becoming qualified does not automatically tell you how to present yourself.

You may know how to support clients while still finding it difficult to explain who you are, which people you work best with and what makes your practice different from the hundreds of others offering therapy.

01

Who am I as a therapist?

Your qualifications describe your training. They do not fully communicate your personality, values, strengths or the experience of working with you.

02

Who do I naturally work well with?

You may not want to force yourself into a narrow niche, but you still need to recognise the people, difficulties and ways of working that suit you.

03

What makes my practice recognisable?

Without clarity, therapist websites often fall back on the same language, imagery and promises, making capable practitioners difficult to distinguish.

04

How much of myself should I show?

A professional identity does not need to become a performance. The aim is to communicate enough of yourself for suitable clients to recognise a fit.

05

What should my website actually say?

You do not need to arrive with perfect copy. The language comes from understanding your practice more clearly before trying to market it.

06

How do I become visible without becoming salesy?

Ethical visibility is not about becoming louder. It is about making it easier for the right people to understand and approach you.

The website

The website comes last for a reason.

A website cannot create clarity that does not yet exist. It can only organise, express and reinforce the understanding developed before it.

By the time the website is built, its structure, wording, imagery and visual tone already have a reason. The result is not merely polished. It feels coherent because every part comes from the same foundation.

FAQ

Questions therapists often ask.

Is this only for newly qualified therapists?

No. The process is particularly useful for newly qualified and early-career therapists who are forming their first professional identity. I also work with established practitioners whose current brand or website no longer reflects the therapist they have become.

Do I need to have chosen a niche?

No. Practice Clarity can help you recognise patterns in the people, difficulties and ways of working that naturally suit you. The aim is to create useful focus without forcing you into a narrow or artificial label.

Do I need to arrive with my website copy written?

No. You do not need perfect wording before the process begins. Your core messaging is developed from the deeper understanding of your practice, audience, values and way of working.

Can I begin with Practice Clarity only?

Yes. Practice Clarity can stand alone and give you a written foundation for your current website, directory profile, social media, future branding or wider private-practice decisions.

Do you use website templates?

I use reliable technical foundations and established therapeutic user journeys where they improve quality and efficiency. The positioning, language, identity, emphasis and emotional experience are shaped around the individual therapist rather than selected from a generic finished design.

Will I need to replace everything again in a few years?

The aim is to create a strategy-led foundation that can grow with your practice. Your work will naturally evolve, but a clear positioning and coherent identity should usually need thoughtful refinement rather than repeated reinvention.
