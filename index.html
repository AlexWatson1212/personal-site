Why most websites feel generic

The problem usually isn't the website.

Most helping professionals know how they work. What is much harder is translating that into a clear message, visual direction and online presence that genuinely feels like them.

A website can look polished and still feel vague. The deeper questions are often about identity, trust and communication.

What makes you different? Who are you trying to reach? What should someone feel when they land on your site? What makes your work trustworthy? What tone, structure, colours and images reflect who you are?

The website is the outcome. Clarity comes first.

About

Understanding people first. Building websites second.

My background is in marketing, branding and communication. I have a degree in Marketing Psychology and over ten years of experience helping businesses think about how they are seen, understood and trusted.

I have worked with businesses across a wide range of industries, from engineering and beauty to hospitality, retail and local service businesses. This variety has taught me how to quickly understand what matters to both businesses and the people they serve.

I originally learned website design as a practical way to bring branding, positioning and communication together. The website became the place where strategy could be seen, felt and experienced.

I am also training as a counsellor, which has deepened my interest in trust, human behaviour and meaningful communication. That combination shapes the way I work: calm design, clear messaging and websites that feel honest, grounded and human.

Clear message. Calm design. Human trust. A website should not just look professional. It should help people understand who you are and feel confident taking the next step.
