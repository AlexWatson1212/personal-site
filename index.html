Brand clarity & websites for therapists

Therapists do not need better marketing. They need to be understood.

The belief

A good therapy website should not perform. It should reveal.

Most therapist websites begin with colours, layouts and generic copy. I start with understanding: who you are, who you help, what your clients need to feel, and what must be communicated clearly before they make contact.

01

The Personal Brand Blueprint.

The foundation of every project is the Personal Brand Blueprint — a clear, carefully written document that captures who you are, who you help, how you work, and how your practice should be understood.

It is not a questionnaire, a moodboard or a collection of notes. It is a carefully interpreted reflection of your professional identity, grounded in what you have actually said, shown and demonstrated.

02

Every recommendation has a reason.

The Blueprint is built around one principle: nothing should appear on your website unless it can be traced back to something real you said, showed or demonstrated.

Evidence

What you said, repeated, demonstrated or clearly cared about during the Brand Clarity Session.

Interpretation

The patterns, themes and strategic meaning drawn from that evidence.

Implication

What that means for your message, structure, design and website journey.

Website decision

The final wording, page structure, visual direction or call to action that follows from the strategy.

03

Six questions before design.

These are the questions that stop your website becoming vague, generic or difficult to recognise yourself in.

01

Who are you?

Your story, values, working style, philosophy and professional identity.

02

Who do you help?

The people your website is speaking to and the state they may be in when they arrive.

03

What change do you help create?

The emotional and practical transformation your work supports.

04

Why would someone choose you?

Your strengths, perspective, fit, boundaries and genuine positioning.

05

How should people experience you?

The tone, atmosphere, trust signals and emotional quality of the brand.

06

What should someone do next?

The website journey, calls to action, enquiry experience and content priorities.

11

Questions people often ask.

Do I need an existing website?

No. Some people come with an existing website that no longer reflects their work. Others are starting completely from scratch.

Can I start with the Personal Brand Blueprint only?

Yes. The Blueprint can stand alone. You can use it to clarify your direction, improve your current website, or decide whether a full redesign is the right next step.

What happens in the Brand Clarity Session?

We talk through your story, values, clients, working style, positioning and website needs. The session is designed to draw out the material your Blueprint and website will be built from.

How is the Blueprint evidence-based?

The Blueprint is based on what you say, repeat, emphasise and demonstrate during the Brand Clarity process. The aim is not to invent a brand for you, but to identify what is already there and translate it into clear communication.

Will I see the reasoning behind recommendations?

Yes. The process is designed so recommendations can be traced back to evidence, interpretation and practical implications for your website. That means decisions are not made simply because something sounds good or looks attractive.

Do you use AI during the process?

I may use carefully supervised tools to help organise notes, transcripts and early draft material. The judgement, interpretation, recommendations and final direction are reviewed and shaped by me. AI does not replace the listening, thinking or decision-making.

What if I don't know what I want to say?

That is one of the most common reasons people get in touch. You do not need to arrive with perfect wording. The process is designed to help us find it.

Can you help with the wording?

Yes. Messaging is central to the work. We shape the language before and during the design process, so the website is not just attractive but clear.

How long does a project usually take?

Most complete website projects take around four to six weeks from first conversation to launch, depending on the size of the site and how quickly feedback is provided.

Do you provide hosting?

Yes. I offer an optional Hosting & Care plan for hosting, maintenance, security updates and support after launch.

Who do you work with?

I mainly work with therapists, counsellors, coaches and helping professionals whose work depends on trust, relationship and careful communication.
