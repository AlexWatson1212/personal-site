How to use the library

One path. Three stages. Nine principles.

The principles are arranged as a connected learning journey. Each stage builds on the previous one, moving from understanding your practice to expressing it clearly and then creating a trustworthy route into contact.

Stage 01 Understand Clarify what your practice is reflecting and how people first experience it. Stage 02 Express Help the right people recognise themselves and understand the therapist behind the work. Stage 03 Connect Make the complete journey simpler, more consistent and easier to enter.

The pathway at a glance

The Practice Clarity pathway

The nine principles.

Read them in order for the complete pathway, or return to the principle that matches the question you are currently trying to answer.

01

Understand the practice

Begin with what people are already experiencing.

Before writing copy or choosing how to promote yourself, understand what your practice reflects, how people encounter it and what helps them feel safe enough to stay.

Principle 01

The Mirror Principle

Your website can only reflect the understanding, personality and values already present within your practice.

Read principle →

Principle 02

The Waiting Room Principle

Your online presence begins shaping familiarity and trust before somebody is ready to contact you.

Read principle →

Principle 03

The Threshold Principle

Your website should reduce enough uncertainty that somebody feels safe enough to stay and look more closely.

Read principle →

02

Express the practice

Help the right person recognise themselves and understand you.

Once somebody feels safe enough to stay, the next task is to make the practice relevant, understandable and human without turning it into a performance.

Principle 04

The Recognition Principle

The right clients are more likely to respond when your language helps them recognise their experience and feel understood.

Read principle →

Principle 05

The Homepage Principle

Your homepage should quickly help the right person understand whether they are in the right place and where to go next.

Read principle →

Principle 06

The About Principle

Your About page should reveal enough of the therapist for somebody to imagine beginning a conversation.

Read principle →

03

Connect the experience

Make the complete journey feel clear and coherent.

The final stage removes unnecessary complexity, creates consistency across each touchpoint and makes the move from interest to enquiry feel easier.

Principle 07

The Simplicity Principle

Clarity grows when every page has one primary responsibility and anything unnecessary is removed.

Read principle →

Principle 08

The Consistency Principle

Trust strengthens when your profile, website, imagery and communication all feel like expressions of the same practice.

Read principle →

Principle 09

The Enquiry Principle

A clear enquiry process reduces uncertainty and helps both people decide whether beginning together may feel right.

Read principle →
