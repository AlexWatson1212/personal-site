Practice Clarity Library

Understand your practice. Then learn how to communicate it.

A step-by-step learning path for therapists who want to define their professional identity, explain their work clearly and build a practice the right clients can recognise and trust.

Start the nine principles Browse practical resources →

New to marketing? Follow the principles in order. Already working on your practice? Begin with the stage that feels unclear.

How to use the library

One path. Three stages. Nine principles.

The library is organised like a short course. Each stage builds on the one before it, taking you from self-understanding to clear communication and then into a trustworthy client experience.

Stage 01 Define Understand who you are and what shapes your work. Stage 02 Communicate Turn that understanding into clear, recognisable language. Stage 03 Connect Create a coherent journey from discovery to enquiry.

The Practice Clarity pathway

The nine principles.

Read them in sequence for a complete foundation, or return to the principle that matches the decision you are currently trying to make.

01

Define your practice

Begin with the therapist, not the marketing.

Before choosing a niche, writing a homepage or thinking about visibility, clarify the values, qualities and experiences already present in your work.

Principle 01

The Mirror Principle

Your practice should reflect the real quality, personality and values of your work—not a generic idea of what therapy is supposed to look like.

Read principle →

Principle 02

The Recognition Principle

The right clients are more likely to respond when your language helps them recognise themselves and feel understood.

Read principle →

Principle 03

The Simplicity Principle

Clarity comes from deciding what matters most, then removing anything that makes the message harder to understand.

Read principle →

02

Communicate your practice

Translate self-understanding into clear language.

Once the foundation is clearer, shape the pages and messages that help a potential client understand your practice without asking them to decode it.

Principle 04

The Homepage Principle

Your homepage should quickly orient the right person: who you help, what you understand and where they should go next.

Read principle →

Principle 05

The About Principle

Your About page is not a biography. It is where relevant parts of your story help a client understand your perspective and presence.

Read principle →

Principle 06

The Consistency Principle

Trust grows when your profile, website, imagery and first contact all feel like expressions of the same practice.

Read principle →

03

Connect with the right clients

Build a coherent journey from discovery to enquiry.

Visibility is useful only when it leads into an experience that feels calm, connected and trustworthy for the person deciding whether to contact you.

Principle 07

The Waiting Room Principle

Your content and online presence can begin building familiarity before someone is ready to make contact.

Read principle →

Principle 08

The First Conversation Principle

The first exchange should continue the tone and clarity of the website, not feel like an abrupt change of experience.

Read principle →

Principle 09

The Enquiry Principle

A clear enquiry process reduces uncertainty and helps both therapist and client decide whether the relationship may be a good fit.

Read principle →
